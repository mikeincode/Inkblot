# Inkblot Terminology

This document defines the canonical language of Inkblot.

Inkblot intentionally avoids generic platform terminology in favor of language that reinforces the feeling of participating in living mythology.

Words shape perception.

---

# Core Structure

## Universe
A complete mythology ecosystem.

A Universe contains:
- a Trunk
- Branches
- Timelines
- Nodes
- Chambers
- Factions
- governance systems
- historical lineage

Examples:
- Ashfall Kingdoms
- Neon Hollow
- Eclipse Protocol

---

## Trunk
The primary canon continuity of a Universe.

The Trunk represents:
- official mythology
- accepted narrative continuity
- structured world history

The Trunk grows upward through canonized Nodes.

---

## Branch
A divergence from the Trunk or another Branch.

Branches may represent:
- alternate interpretations
- divergent timelines
- unofficial narratives
- speculative lore
- mutation pathways

Some Branches eventually rival the Trunk itself in cultural importance.

---

## Timeline
The chronological progression of events within a Trunk or Branch.

Timelines organize:
- narrative sequence
- historical development
- divergence points
- evolutionary progression

A Branch may contain multiple Timelines.

---

## Node
A single contribution to a Universe.

Nodes may contain:
- lore
- chapters
- characters
- world events
- myths
- artifacts
- timeline divergences
- mutations

Nodes are the fundamental unit of mythology growth.

---

# Ecosystem Systems

## Tree
The governed mythology structure.

The Tree contains:
- canon continuity
- approved Branches
- official Timelines
- Keeper governance

The Tree prioritizes coherence and intentional worldbuilding.

---

## Shadow
The ungoverned mythology ecosystem.

The Shadow contains:
- unofficial narratives
- mutations
- alternate interpretations
- experimental evolution
- community folklore

The Shadow has no canon.
Only consensus.

---

## Mutation
A transformed or remixed version of an existing Node or narrative concept.

Mutations may:
- reinterpret canon
- alter timelines
- invert mythology
- evolve unofficially through community adoption

Mutations primarily exist within the Shadow ecosystem.

---

## Canonize
The act of approving a Node into official continuity.

Canonization integrates a contribution into:
- the Trunk
- an official Branch
- recognized historical continuity

Canonization is typically controlled by Keepers or governance systems.

---

# Governance & Identity

## Keeper
A Universe maintainer or governance authority.

Keepers may:
- review Nodes
- canonize submissions
- shape the Trunk
- manage Branch legitimacy
- define governance rules

Not all Universes require Keepers.

---

## Governance Model
The structural philosophy controlling how a Universe evolves.

Examples:
- Closed Tree
- Curated Tree
- Open Tree
- Wild Shadow
- Dual Ecology

Governance affects:
- canon permissions
- mutation behavior
- contribution authority
- Shadow interaction

---

# Social Systems

## Chamber
A localized discussion space tied to:
Universe → Branch → Timeline

Chambers are designed to feel like:
- lore halls
- archival councils
- interpretive gatherings
- mythology debates

Not generic forums.

---

## Resonance
A measure of community agreement, impact, or interpretive support.

Resonance replaces traditional social-media “likes.”

The term was chosen to reinforce:
- mythology participation
- interpretive alignment
- cultural influence

rather than shallow engagement.

---

## Voice
A contribution within a Chamber discussion.

Voice replaces the term “comment.”

---

## Faction
An ideological or interpretive group within a Universe.

Factions represent:
- philosophical interpretation
- canon beliefs
- mutation stances
- governance ideology
- narrative schools of thought

Factions are not generic communities.

They are mythology interpretation orders.

---

## Alignment
A faction or contributor’s philosophical orientation toward the ecosystem.

Examples:
- Tree Aligned
- Shadow Aligned
- Dual Aligned
- Unaligned

---

# Design Language

## Living Mythology
The core emotional design principle of Inkblot.

The platform should always feel like:
- evolving worlds
- historical accumulation
- interpretive participation
- narrative ecology

Never:
- corporate
- sterile
- productivity-focused
- generic social media
