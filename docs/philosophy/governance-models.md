# Governance Models

Governance determines how a Universe evolves.

Every mythology ecosystem exists somewhere between:
- preservation
- mutation
- authority
- consensus
- structure
- emergence

Inkblot allows each Universe to define its own narrative governance philosophy.

Governance affects:
- canon authority
- contribution permissions
- Shadow interaction
- mutation acceptance
- branch legitimacy
- historical continuity

Different governance models create fundamentally different mythology ecosystems.

---

# Core Philosophy

No single governance structure is universally correct.

Some worlds thrive through:
- strong canon authority
- historical coherence
- careful curation

Others thrive through:
- unrestricted mutation
- collaborative evolution
- decentralized interpretation

Governance is not moderation alone.

It is narrative philosophy.

---

# Governance Spectrum

Inkblot governance exists on a spectrum between:
- complete canon preservation
and
- unrestricted mythology evolution

Most Universes exist somewhere between these extremes.

---

# Closed Tree

## Philosophy
Canon is sacred.

The Universe maintains:
- strict continuity
- centralized authority
- controlled narrative growth

Only approved Keepers may significantly alter the Trunk.

---

## Characteristics
- highly curated canon
- limited branching
- strict submission review
- low mutation permeability
- minimal Shadow influence

---

## Strengths
- strong continuity
- coherent mythology
- stable historical identity
- high narrative consistency

---

## Risks
- stagnation
- limited experimentation
- community alienation
- rigid interpretation culture

---

# Curated Tree

## Philosophy
Community participation is valuable, but canon requires stewardship.

Contributors may:
- submit Nodes
- propose Branches
- influence timelines

But Keepers maintain final authority.

---

## Characteristics
- active canon review
- controlled branch growth
- moderated Shadow interaction
- selective mutation acceptance

---

## Strengths
- balanced structure
- collaborative growth
- manageable continuity
- strong narrative identity

---

## Risks
- governance disputes
- faction tension
- perceived gatekeeping
- canon bottlenecks

---

# Open Tree

## Philosophy
Canon should evolve collaboratively.

Most contributors may:
- create Nodes
- expand Branches
- influence timelines directly

Governance focuses more on stabilization than restriction.

---

## Characteristics
- decentralized growth
- high contribution volume
- evolving continuity
- broad branch participation

---

## Strengths
- rapid mythology expansion
- strong community ownership
- dynamic ecosystem growth
- high participation energy

---

## Risks
- canon instability
- continuity fragmentation
- mythology overload
- historical incoherence

---

# Wild Shadow

## Philosophy
No centralized canon authority exists.

The Universe evolves entirely through:
- community participation
- resonance
- cultural adoption
- narrative mutation

The Shadow becomes the primary ecosystem.

---

## Characteristics
- unrestricted branching
- unlimited mutation
- consensus legitimacy
- decentralized mythology evolution

---

## Strengths
- maximum creativity
- emergent storytelling
- intense cultural participation
- highly alive mythology

---

## Risks
- structural collapse
- narrative incoherence
- overwhelming complexity
- unstable historical identity

---

# Dual Ecology

## Philosophy
Both canon structure and uncontrolled evolution are necessary.

The Tree and Shadow coexist intentionally.

The Tree preserves:
- continuity
- official history
- narrative structure

The Shadow generates:
- mutation
- experimentation
- alternate truth
- cultural evolution

Neither fully dominates the other.

---

## Characteristics
- active Tree/Shadow interaction
- controlled permeability
- faction politics
- canon negotiation
- evolutionary mythology systems

---

## Strengths
- living mythology
- balanced stability/evolution
- rich community dynamics
- long-term ecosystem resilience

---

## Risks
- ideological conflict
- canon wars
- governance complexity
- faction fragmentation

---

# Governance Roles

## Keepers
Universe maintainers and canon authorities.

Responsibilities may include:
- reviewing submissions
- canonizing Nodes
- managing Branches
- moderating Chambers
- shaping mythology direction

---

## Contributors
Participants who expand mythology through:
- Nodes
- theories
- mutations
- discussions
- timeline growth

---

## Factions
Interpretive ideological groups that influence:
- canon politics
- mutation legitimacy
- narrative direction
- community consensus

---

# Shadow Integration

Governance models differ heavily in how they treat the Shadow.

Possible approaches include:
- total isolation
- monitored coexistence
- selective canonization
- unrestricted integration

Shadow permeability is one of the defining characteristics of a Universe.

---

# Canon Permeability

Canon permeability describes how easily:
- Shadow narratives
- mutations
- unofficial interpretations

can influence official continuity.

High permeability creates:
- evolving canon
- living mythology
- unstable continuity

Low permeability creates:
- strong structure
- stable history
- rigid canon boundaries

---

# Governance & Identity

Governance shapes the emotional identity of a Universe.

Examples:

A Closed Tree may feel:
- ancient
- sacred
- preserved
- authoritative

A Wild Shadow may feel:
- alive
- unstable
- underground
- mythologically chaotic

A Dual Ecology may feel:
- politically dynamic
- historically layered
- culturally contested

Governance is worldbuilding.

---

# Long-Term Evolution

Governance models are not necessarily permanent.

Universes may evolve over time through:
- faction pressure
- cultural shifts
- canon crises
- mythology collapse
- Keeper succession
- Shadow dominance

A Universe’s governance history becomes part of its mythology.

---

# Foundational Principle

Every mythology eventually chooses what it fears more:

Chaos,
or stagnation.
