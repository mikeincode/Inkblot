# Inkblot Social Systems

Inkblot is not designed as a traditional social platform.

The goal is not:
- audience accumulation
- influencer culture
- endless engagement loops
- algorithmic popularity contests

Inkblot is designed around:
- collaborative mythology
- interpretive participation
- cultural identity
- narrative evolution
- localized communities

The social layer exists to support living worlds.

---

# Core Philosophy

Participation in Inkblot should feel like:
- entering a mythology
- joining interpretive movements
- contributing to living history
- debating canon
- shaping evolving worlds

Not:
- posting content into feeds
- collecting followers
- performing for algorithms

The platform prioritizes:
- contribution
- interpretation
- identity
- lineage
- resonance
- cultural influence

---

# Localized Social Structure

Inkblot intentionally avoids one massive global discussion feed.

Social activity is contextual and embedded directly into world structures.

Primary structure:

Universe
→ Branch
→ Timeline
→ Chamber
→ Faction

This creates:
- localized identity
- branch-specific culture
- narrative context
- meaningful participation

Every Branch can develop its own:
- discussions
- ideology
- mythology politics
- interpretive culture

---

# Chambers

Chambers are localized discussion spaces tied directly to:
Universe → Branch → Timeline

Chambers are designed to feel like:
- lore halls
- archive chambers
- underground gatherings
- interpretive councils
- canon war rooms

Not generic forums.

---

# Chamber Discussion Types

## Canon Debate
Discussion regarding official continuity and canon legitimacy.

---

## Lore Theory
Interpretive speculation and world analysis.

---

## Mutation Proposal
Suggestions for alternate lore evolution or Shadow divergence.

---

## Timeline Analysis
Discussion focused on chronology, divergence, and historical consequence.

---

## Branch Strategy
Meta-level discussion regarding branch direction and narrative growth.

---

## Shadow Rumor
Unofficial mythology, folklore, and unstable interpretation.

---

# Resonance

Inkblot replaces “likes” with Resonance.

Resonance represents:
- interpretive agreement
- cultural impact
- mythology influence
- community alignment

The term intentionally avoids shallow social-media framing.

Resonance is not popularity.

It is narrative gravity.

---

# Voices

Replies and discussion contributions are called Voices.

The term reinforces the idea that:
mythology evolves through collective participation and interpretation.

---

# Factions

Factions are ideological interpretation orders.

They are not generic groups or communities.

Factions represent:
- mythology philosophy
- canon ideology
- governance beliefs
- mutation stance
- interpretive alignment

Examples:
- Canon Preservationists
- Shadow Loyalists
- Divergence Theorists
- Rootkeepers
- Free Forkers

---

# Alignment Systems

Users and Factions may align philosophically with:

## Tree Alignment
Prioritizes canon continuity and structured mythology.

---

## Shadow Alignment
Prioritizes mutation, reinterpretation, and narrative evolution.

---

## Dual Alignment
Believes both canon and mutation are necessary for mythology growth.

---

## Unaligned
Participates without formal ideological attachment.

---

# Identity Through Participation

Inkblot users are not passive audiences.

Participants may become:
- Chroniclers
- Keepers
- Archivists
- Mythsmiths
- Wanderers
- Mutationists
- Faction Leaders

Identity is earned through contribution and participation.

---

# Contribution History

Every participant develops a visible mythology lineage through:
- Nodes contributed
- Branches influenced
- Mutations created
- Chambers participated in
- Factions joined
- Canonizations achieved

Profiles are designed to feel like:
- archival identity records
- mythology histories
- contribution maps

Not influencer dashboards.

---

# Canon Politics

Inkblot intentionally allows ideological tension.

Communities naturally generate:
- competing interpretations
- canon disputes
- faction conflicts
- unofficial truths
- mythology politics

These systems are not flaws.

They are part of living mythology.

---

# Shadow Social Ecology

The Shadow acts as narrative natural selection.

Within the Shadow:
- ideas compete
- mutations evolve
- timelines diverge
- unofficial canon forms through consensus

Some Shadow narratives may eventually become culturally stronger than official canon.

---

# Social Design Rules

Inkblot should NEVER feel like:
- generic social media
- corporate productivity software
- modern influencer platforms
- engagement-maximizing feeds

The platform should ALWAYS feel like:
- living mythology
- evolving history
- interpretive civilization
- collaborative world memory
