# Future Ideas

This document is the long-term idea vault for Inkblot.

Not every concept here should be built immediately.

Some ideas may:
- evolve
- merge
- disappear
- become foundational later

The purpose of this document is to preserve creative momentum and system discoveries over time.

Inkblot is designed as an evolving mythology ecosystem.
Its future systems should emerge naturally from that philosophy.

---

# Living Mythology Systems

## Consensus Canon Tracking
Track when Shadow narratives become more culturally influential than official canon.

Possible indicators:
- Resonance
- faction adoption
- Chamber activity
- branch growth velocity
- mutation spread

Potential outcome:
Shadow branches can become “Consensus Canon.”

---

## Historical Drift
Older Universes slowly evolve cultural reinterpretation over time.

Examples:
- ancient Nodes gain mythic status
- timelines become disputed
- contradictory lore accumulates
- forgotten Branches resurface

Universes should feel historically layered.

---

## Mythology Archaeology
Allow contributors to discover:
- buried Nodes
- archived timelines
- abandoned branches
- forgotten factions
- extinct interpretations

Exploration becomes part of participation.

---

# Shadow Evolution Systems

## Mutation Chains
Mutations can recursively mutate other Mutations.

Creates:
- lineage trees
- folklore evolution
- narrative species
- ideological divergence chains

---

## Mutation Infection
A powerful Mutation can begin influencing nearby timelines and branches.

Possible effects:
- visual corruption
- ideology spread
- faction conversion
- narrative destabilization

The Shadow behaves like cultural evolution.

---

## Shadow Collapse Events
Some Shadow ecosystems become too unstable and collapse into fragmentation.

Could create:
- mythology extinction
- branch ruins
- archival recovery events
- reconstruction efforts
